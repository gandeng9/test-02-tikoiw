Job Hiring In Canada With Lmia: What You Need To Know In 2023

Introduction

Are you planning to work in Canada but unsure about the requirements for job hiring? One essential document to secure is the Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA). In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about job hiring in Canada with LMIA in 2023.

What is LMIA?

LMIA is a document issued by the Government of Canada that assesses the impact of foreign workers on the Canadian labour market. It is a crucial document that employers must secure before they can hire foreign workers. The LMIA ensures that Canadians have the first opportunity to apply for available job positions before foreign workers.

Who Needs LMIA?

Employers who want to hire foreign workers in Canada need to secure LMIA. It applies to all temporary foreign workers, including those who are under the International Mobility Program. The only exception is for workers who are exempted from LMIA requirements, such as NAFTA Professionals, Intra-Company Transferees, and Spouses and Common-Law Partners.

How to Get LMIA?

Employers need to apply for LMIA through the Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC). They need to provide information about the job position, including the job duties, qualifications, and salary. They also need to show that they have tried to recruit Canadians but could not find suitable candidates.

LMIA Processing Time

The LMIA processing time varies depending on the type of application and location. In general, it takes around ten to twelve weeks to process a standard LMIA application. However, employers can also use the Global Talent Stream program, which has a faster processing time of two weeks.

LMIA Fees

Employers need to pay a processing fee of CAD 1,000 for each LMIA application. They also need to provide a CAD 100 compliance fee per foreign worker. However, some exemptions apply, such as for workers under the Global Talent Stream program.

Job Hiring Process with LMIA

Once the employer has secured the LMIA, they can now offer the job position to the foreign worker. The worker then needs to apply for a work permit and submit the LMIA as a supporting document. The work permit processing time varies depending on the location, but it usually takes around four to six weeks.

LMIA Renewal

LMIA is valid for six months from the date of issuance. Employers who want to hire the same foreign worker after the LMIA has expired need to apply for a new LMIA. They need to show that the job position is still available, and they could not find suitable Canadians to fill the job.

Conclusion

Securing LMIA is a crucial step for employers who want to hire foreign workers in Canada. It ensures that Canadians have the first opportunity to apply for available job positions. As a foreign worker, it is essential to know the LMIA requirements and process to increase your chances of getting hired in Canada.
