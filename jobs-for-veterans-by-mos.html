Introduction

As of 2023, veterans have been an integral part of the US workforce, bringing a wealth of experience, discipline, and transferable skills to the job market. However, finding the right job can be challenging, especially with the wide range of military occupational specialties (MOS) available. This article aims to provide insight into jobs for veterans by MOS.

Administration and Support

Human Resources Specialist (HR)

HR specialists are responsible for recruitment, personnel management, and administrative support. Veterans with MOS 42A (Human Resources Specialist) are well-suited for this role, having experience in managing personnel records, processing benefits, and conducting interviews.

Logistics Specialist

With MOS 92A (Automated Logistical Specialist), veterans have experience in supply chain management, inventory control, and logistics support. These skills can be applied to civilian roles in logistics companies or in supply chain management positions.

Engineering and Technical

Mechanic

Veterans with MOS 91B (Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic) have experience in maintaining and repairing vehicles, which translates well to civilian roles in the automotive industry. This MOS can also be applied to roles in maintenance and repair of industrial equipment.

IT Specialist

With MOS 25B (Information Technology Specialist), veterans have experience in installing, maintaining, and troubleshooting computer systems and networks. This MOS can be applied to civilian roles in IT support, network administration, and cybersecurity.

Healthcare

Combat Medic

Veterans with MOS 68W (Combat Medic) have experience in emergency medical treatment, first aid, and trauma care. These skills can be applied to civilian roles in healthcare, such as emergency medical technicians, paramedics, and nursing assistants.

Medical Laboratory Specialist

With MOS 68K (Medical Laboratory Specialist), veterans have experience in conducting laboratory tests and analyzing results. This MOS can be applied to civilian roles in medical and research laboratories.

Construction and Maintenance

Carpenter

Veterans with MOS 12W (Carpentry and Masonry Specialist) have experience in constructing and repairing buildings, which translates well to civilian roles in construction and maintenance. This MOS can also be applied to roles in woodworking and furniture making.

Electrician

With MOS 12R (Interior Electrician), veterans have experience in installing and repairing electrical systems. This MOS can be applied to civilian roles in electrical contracting, construction, and maintenance.

Conclusion

As the job market evolves, veterans with MOS possess transferable skills that can be applied to a variety of civilian roles. By understanding the job opportunities available for each MOS, veterans can make informed decisions about their career paths and find fulfilling jobs that make use of their experience and skills.
