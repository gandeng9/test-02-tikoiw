Introduction: The Legacy of Steve Jobs

In 2023, it’s been over a decade since the passing of Steve Jobs, the visionary behind Apple Inc. Jobs was not only a co-founder of Apple but also the mastermind behind some of the company’s most iconic products, such as the iPhone, iPad, and MacBook. Even after his death, Jobs’ influence on Apple and the tech industry as a whole remains significant. One aspect of Jobs’ relationship with Apple that is often discussed is his percentage of ownership in the company.

Early Days: Co-Founding Apple

When Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak co-founded Apple in 1976, they each owned 45% of the company, and the other 10% was owned by Ronald Wayne. However, after Wayne sold his share back to Jobs and Wozniak just a few months later, Jobs’ ownership percentage increased to 50%.

The Rise of Apple: Jobs’ Ownership Percentage Grows

As Apple grew in popularity and success, Jobs’ ownership percentage increased. By 1985, Jobs’ ownership had reached 15%, and he was the largest individual shareholder in the company. However, that same year, Jobs resigned from Apple following a power struggle with then-CEO John Sculley.

The Return of Jobs: Regaining Control

In 1997, Apple was struggling financially, and Jobs returned to the company as interim CEO. At that time, Jobs owned just 1.5% of Apple, but he quickly began to turn the company around. He introduced a series of successful products, such as the iMac, and in 2000, he was officially named CEO. By 2003, Jobs’ ownership percentage had grown to 5.5%.

The iPhone Era: Jobs’ Ownership at its Peak

The launch of the iPhone in 2007 marked a new era for Apple, and it was during this time that Jobs’ ownership percentage reached its peak. In 2008, Jobs owned approximately 5.5 million shares of Apple, which equated to around 0.5% of the company. However, the success of the iPhone and other Apple products continued to drive up the company’s value, and Jobs’ net worth continued to rise.

The End of an Era: Jobs’ Passing

On October 5, 2011, Steve Jobs passed away at the age of 56. At the time of his death, Jobs owned 5.5 million shares of Apple, which were worth approximately $2.1 billion. While Jobs’ ownership percentage had decreased over the years, his influence on Apple and the tech industry was immeasurable.

Conclusion: Jobs’ Legacy at Apple

In the years since Jobs’ passing, Apple has continued to thrive, thanks in part to the foundation he laid during his time at the company. Jobs’ percentage of ownership in Apple may have fluctuated over the years, but his impact on the company and the tech industry as a whole cannot be overstated. As we move into the future, it’s clear that Jobs’ legacy will continue to shape the way we think about technology and innovation.
