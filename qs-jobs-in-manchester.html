Qs Jobs In Manchester: Opportunities And Challenges

Introduction

Manchester, one of the largest cities in the UK, has been a hub for business and employment for decades. The city is home to various industries, including finance, healthcare, and technology. One of the growing job sectors in Manchester is quantity surveying (QS). In this article, we will discuss the opportunities and challenges of QS jobs in Manchester.

What is Quantity Surveying?

Quantity surveying is a specialized field that involves managing costs and contracts on construction projects. A quantity surveyor is responsible for estimating the cost of materials, labor, and equipment required for a project, preparing tender documents, and negotiating contracts with suppliers and subcontractors.

Opportunities in QS Jobs in Manchester

Manchester is a city that is constantly growing and developing, with new construction projects popping up all the time. This means that there are plenty of opportunities for QS professionals to find work. Some of the top employers for QS jobs in Manchester include construction companies, engineering firms, and architectural firms.

Challenges in QS Jobs in Manchester

While there are plenty of opportunities for QS professionals in Manchester, there are also some challenges to be aware of. One of the biggest challenges is the competition for jobs. With so many construction projects happening in the city, there are a lot of other QS professionals vying for the same positions.

Skills Required for QS Jobs in Manchester

To be successful in a QS job in Manchester, there are several key skills that you should have. These include:
  • Excellent analytical and mathematical skills
  • Strong communication and negotiation skills
  • Attention to detail and accuracy
  • Ability to work well under pressure and meet deadlines
  • Understanding of building regulations and construction contracts

Training and Qualifications for QS Jobs in Manchester

To become a QS professional, you will need to have a degree in quantity surveying or a related field. Many employers also prefer candidates who have completed a professional qualification, such as the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) Assessment of Professional Competence (APC).

Salary Expectations for QS Jobs in Manchester

The salary for QS jobs in Manchester can vary depending on your level of experience and qualifications. Entry-level QS professionals can expect to earn around £25,000 per year, while senior QS professionals with several years of experience can earn upwards of £60,000 per year.

Conclusion

If you are looking for a challenging and rewarding career in the construction industry, then QS jobs in Manchester may be the perfect fit for you. With plenty of opportunities available and a strong demand for skilled professionals, this is a field that is worth exploring further. By developing the necessary skills and qualifications, you can position yourself for success in this exciting and dynamic industry.
