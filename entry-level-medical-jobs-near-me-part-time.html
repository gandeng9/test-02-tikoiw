Entry Level Medical Jobs Near Me Part Time

Introduction

Finding a part-time job in the medical field can be a great way to gain experience and earn extra income. Whether you're a student looking for a job to fit around your studies or someone looking to switch careers, there are plenty of entry-level medical jobs available near you.

1. Medical Receptionist

One of the most popular entry-level medical jobs is a medical receptionist. As a receptionist, you'll be responsible for answering phones, scheduling appointments, and greeting patients. This job doesn't require any previous experience, but having excellent customer service skills is a must.

2. Medical Transcriptionist

As a medical transcriptionist, you'll be responsible for transcribing medical records and other documents. This job requires excellent typing skills and attention to detail. While previous experience is preferred, many employers offer on-the-job training.

3. Medical Biller

Medical billing is another entry-level job that can be done part-time. As a medical biller, you'll be responsible for submitting claims to insurance companies and following up on payments. This job requires knowledge of medical terminology and billing codes.

4. Medical Assistant

A medical assistant is responsible for performing clinical and administrative tasks in a medical office. This job requires a certification or diploma from a medical assistant program. However, many employers offer on-the-job training.

5. Phlebotomist

As a phlebotomist, you'll be responsible for drawing blood from patients for medical tests and donations. This job requires a certification or diploma from a phlebotomy program. However, some employers offer on-the-job training.

6. Home Health Aide

Home health aides provide in-home care for elderly or disabled individuals. This job requires a certification from a home health aide program. However, some employers offer on-the-job training.

7. Medical Scribe

As a medical scribe, you'll be responsible for documenting patient information for physicians. This job requires excellent typing skills and knowledge of medical terminology. While previous experience is preferred, many employers offer on-the-job training.

8. Medical Records Clerk

As a medical records clerk, you'll be responsible for organizing and maintaining patient records. This job requires excellent organizational skills and knowledge of medical terminology.

9. Medical Courier

As a medical courier, you'll be responsible for transporting medical supplies and specimens between healthcare facilities. This job requires a driver's license and a clean driving record.

10. Medical Equipment Technician

As a medical equipment technician, you'll be responsible for installing, maintaining, and repairing medical equipment. This job requires a certification or diploma from a medical equipment technician program. However, some employers offer on-the-job training.

Conclusion

There are many entry-level medical jobs available near you that can be done part-time. Whether you're looking to gain experience or earn extra income, there's a job out there for you. Consider your skills and interests when choosing a job and don't be afraid to ask for on-the-job training.
