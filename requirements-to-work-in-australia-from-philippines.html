Requirements To Work In Australia From Philippines

Introduction

Australia has always been a top destination for Filipinos who want to work abroad. The country offers a high standard of living, excellent job opportunities, and a multicultural society that is welcoming to immigrants. However, working in Australia requires meeting certain requirements that Filipinos need to be aware of. In this article, we will discuss the requirements to work in Australia from the Philippines.

Visa Requirements

The first requirement for working in Australia is obtaining a work visa. Filipinos can apply for a work visa through the Australian Embassy in Manila or online. There are several types of work visas available, depending on the nature of the job and the length of stay. The most common type of work visa is the Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) visa, which allows workers to stay in Australia for up to four years.

Qualifications and Skills

To be eligible for a work visa, Filipinos must have the necessary qualifications and skills required for the job. For example, if applying for a job as an engineer, the applicant must have a degree in engineering and relevant work experience. It is essential to research the job market and find out what skills and qualifications are in demand to increase your chances of getting a job offer.

English Language Proficiency

Australia is an English-speaking country, and proficiency in the English language is essential for working and living in the country. Filipinos must provide proof of their English language proficiency when applying for a work visa. The minimum requirement for most work visas is a score of 5 on the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) test.

Health and Character Requirements

Filipinos must meet certain health and character requirements to work in Australia. They must undergo a medical examination and provide police clearance certificates from the Philippines and any other country they have lived in for more than 12 months in the last ten years. Any health or character issues may affect their eligibility for a work visa.

Employment Contract

Before applying for a work visa, Filipinos must have a job offer from an Australian employer. The employment contract must include details such as salary, working hours, and job description. It is important to ensure that the contract complies with Australian employment laws.

Salary and Benefits

Australia has a minimum wage system, and employers must pay their workers according to the minimum wage rates. Filipinos must ensure that their salary meets the minimum wage requirements for their occupation. They must also be aware of their entitlements to benefits such as superannuation, sick leave, and annual leave.

Housing and Accommodation

Finding suitable accommodation is essential when working in Australia. Filipinos must research the cost of living and housing in their chosen location and budget accordingly. It is advisable to secure temporary accommodation before arriving in Australia and then look for permanent housing once settled.

Transportation and Commuting

Australia has an excellent public transportation system, and Filipinos can choose to use buses, trains, or trams to travel to work. It is important to research the commuting options and costs and factor them into the budget.

Cultural Adjustment

Australia has a diverse and multicultural society, but adjusting to a new culture can be challenging. Filipinos must be prepared to adapt to different customs and ways of life. It is advisable to learn about Australian culture before arriving and seek support from Filipino communities in Australia.

Conclusion

Working in Australia offers many opportunities for Filipinos, but it requires meeting certain requirements. By understanding the visa requirements, qualifications and skills, English language proficiency, health and character requirements, and employment contract terms, Filipinos can increase their chances of success. It is also essential to research the cost of living, housing, transportation, and cultural adjustment to ensure a smooth transition to working and living in Australia.
