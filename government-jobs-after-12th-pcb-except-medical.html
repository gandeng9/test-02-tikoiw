Government Jobs After 12Th Pcb Except Medical

Introduction

After completing 12th with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, students usually opt for medical courses. However, not all students are interested in pursuing medicine. Many students want to explore other career options after 12th PCB. In this article, we will discuss the various government job options available for students after 12th PCB except medical.

1. Agricultural Officer

Agriculture is a field that is often overlooked by students after 12th PCB. However, it is a field that offers many job opportunities. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Agriculture and then apply for the post of Agricultural Officer in government organizations like the Agriculture Department, Food Corporation of India, or National Seeds Corporation.

2. Environmental Scientist

For students interested in the environment and sustainability, a career as an environmental scientist is a great option. Environmental scientists work with government organizations to ensure that environmental regulations are followed. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Environmental Science and then apply for jobs in organizations like the Ministry of Environment and Forests, Pollution Control Board, or State Forest Department.

3. Forensic Scientist

Forensic Science is an interesting field that involves the application of scientific principles to the investigation of crimes. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Forensic Science and then apply for jobs in organizations like the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI), Intelligence Bureau (IB), or State Police Department.

4. Biotechnologist

Biotechnology is a field that combines biology and technology to develop innovative solutions. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Biotechnology and then apply for jobs in government organizations like the National Centre for Cell Science, National Institute of Biomedical Genomics, or National Institute of Immunology.

5. Wildlife Inspector

For students interested in wildlife conservation, a career as a wildlife inspector is a great option. Wildlife inspectors work with government organizations to protect endangered species and enforce wildlife protection laws. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Zoology or Wildlife Biology and then apply for jobs in organizations like the Ministry of Environment and Forests, Wildlife Crime Control Bureau, or Wildlife Institute of India.

6. Food Inspector

Food safety is an important issue, and the government has strict regulations in place to ensure that food is safe for consumption. Food inspectors work with government organizations to enforce these regulations. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Food Technology or Food Science and then apply for jobs in organizations like the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI), Food Corporation of India, or State Food and Drug Administration.

7. Geologist

Geology is a field that deals with the study of the Earth's physical structure, composition, and history. Geologists work with government organizations to explore and manage natural resources like minerals, oil, and gas. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Geology and then apply for jobs in organizations like the Geological Survey of India, Oil and Natural Gas Corporation, or State Mining Department.

8. Fisheries Inspector

For students interested in marine life, a career as a fisheries inspector is a great option. Fisheries inspectors work with government organizations to ensure that fishing practices are sustainable and that fish populations are protected. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Fisheries Science and then apply for jobs in organizations like the Marine Products Export Development Authority, Central Institute of Fisheries Technology, or State Fisheries Department.

9. Statistician

Statistics is a field that deals with the collection, analysis, and interpretation of data. Statisticians work with government organizations to analyze data and provide insights that help with policy decisions. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Statistics and then apply for jobs in organizations like the National Sample Survey Office, Central Statistical Office, or State Planning Commission.

10. Biomedical Engineer

Biomedical Engineering is a field that combines engineering and biology to develop medical devices and equipment. Biomedical engineers work with government organizations to develop innovative solutions that improve healthcare. Students can pursue a Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering and then apply for jobs in organizations like the All India Institute of Medical Sciences, National Institute of Health and Family Welfare, or State Health Department.

Conclusion

There are many government job opportunities available for students after 12th PCB except medical. Students should explore different fields and choose a career that aligns with their interests and passions. Pursuing a Bachelor's degree in a relevant field is the first step towards a fulfilling career in the government sector.
