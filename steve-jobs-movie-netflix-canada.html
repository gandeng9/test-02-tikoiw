The Background

In 2015, the world saw the release of the biographical movie 'Steve Jobs' directed by Danny Boyle and written by Aaron Sorkin. The movie showcases the life of the Apple co-founder, Steve Jobs, and his journey to creating one of the most successful companies in the world. The movie was widely acclaimed for its direction, script, and performances, receiving numerous nominations and awards. In 2023, the movie is now available on Netflix Canada, and we decided to take a closer look.

The Plot

The movie is divided into three parts, each taking place before a major product launch by Apple. The first part takes place in 1984 before the launch of the first Macintosh computer, the second part in 1988 before the launch of NeXT, and the final part in 1998 before the launch of the iMac. The plot revolves around the relationships between Jobs and his colleagues, including Steve Wozniak, John Sculley, and Joanna Hoffman. It also delves into Jobs' personal life, including his relationships with his daughter and her mother.

The Performances

The movie boasts of an impressive cast, with Michael Fassbender playing the role of Steve Jobs. Fassbender's performance is captivating, and he manages to portray Jobs' personality and mannerisms flawlessly. Kate Winslet plays Joanna Hoffman, Jobs' confidante and marketing chief, and delivers a powerful performance. Other notable performances include Seth Rogen as Steve Wozniak and Jeff Daniels as John Sculley.

The Direction and Script

Danny Boyle's direction and Aaron Sorkin's script are the highlights of the movie. Boyle's use of long takes and close-ups creates an immersive experience for the viewer, and Sorkin's dialogue is witty and engaging. The movie manages to keep the viewer hooked throughout its runtime, even though it primarily takes place in the backstage of product launches.

The Verdict

In conclusion, the Steve Jobs movie on Netflix Canada is a must-watch for anyone interested in the story of Apple and its co-founder. The performances, direction, and script are top-notch, and the movie manages to capture the essence of Jobs' personality and vision. While some aspects of the movie may not be entirely accurate, it is still a compelling watch and a testament to the genius of Steve Jobs.
