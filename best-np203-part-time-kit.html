The NP203 Transfer Case: A Brief Overview

If you own a 4x4 truck, chances are you've heard of the NP203 transfer case. This heavy-duty, gear-driven transfer case was used in many popular trucks and SUVs in the 1980s and 1990s, including the Chevy K5 Blazer, Dodge Ramcharger, and Ford Bronco. While the NP203 was known for its durability and strength, many off-road enthusiasts found it to be too heavy and too complex for their needs. That's where the NP203 part time kit comes in.

What Is an NP203 Part Time Kit?

An NP203 part time kit is a set of aftermarket components that allow you to convert your NP203 transfer case from full-time to part-time operation. This means that you can disengage the front driveshaft when you're not using four-wheel drive, which reduces wear and tear on your drivetrain and improves fuel economy. Part time kits usually include a new chain, sprockets, and shift fork, as well as instructions for installation.

The Benefits of Installing an NP203 Part Time Kit

There are several benefits to installing an NP203 part time kit on your truck. First and foremost, you'll be able to save money on gas by disengaging the front driveshaft when you don't need four-wheel drive. This can add up to significant savings over time, especially if you do a lot of highway driving. Additionally, a part time kit can reduce wear and tear on your drivetrain, which can extend the life of your truck and save you money on repairs.
