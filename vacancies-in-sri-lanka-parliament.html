Vacancies In Sri Lanka Parliament

Introduction

The Sri Lankan Parliament is the highest legislative body in the country. It consists of elected representatives from various political parties. The parliament plays a crucial role in the governance of the country. Recently, there have been vacancies in the parliament due to various reasons. This article aims to provide an overview of the vacancies in the Sri Lankan parliament and its impact.

Vacancies due to Resignations

One of the reasons for the vacancies in the parliament is due to resignations. Several members have resigned from their positions due to personal reasons or disagreements with their party. These resignations have created a vacuum in the parliament and have affected the functioning of the government.

Impact on Governance

The vacancies in the parliament have affected the governance of the country. With fewer members in the parliament, it becomes challenging to pass important bills and make critical decisions. The government has to rely on the support of opposition parties to pass bills, which can be a time-consuming process.

Vacancies due to Deaths

Another reason for vacancies in the parliament is due to the unfortunate deaths of members. In the past year, several members have passed away due to various reasons. These vacancies have created a void in the parliament and have affected the representation of certain regions.

By-Elections

In the case of vacancies due to deaths, the government has to hold by-elections to fill the vacant seats. By-elections are held in the respective regions, and voters elect a new representative to the parliament. These by-elections can be costly and time-consuming, and it takes time for the newly elected members to settle into their roles.

Vacancies due to Disqualifications

Members of parliament can be disqualified due to various reasons, such as corruption, criminal convictions, or breach of parliamentary privileges. These disqualifications can lead to vacancies in the parliament and affect the functioning of the government.

Impact on Accountability

The disqualification of members of parliament can affect the accountability of the government. With fewer members in the parliament, it becomes challenging to hold the government accountable for its actions. The opposition parties have to rely on other means to hold the government accountable, such as protests and media coverage.

Conclusion

In conclusion, vacancies in the Sri Lankan parliament have a significant impact on the governance of the country. The government has to rely on the support of opposition parties to pass bills and make critical decisions. By-elections can be costly and time-consuming, and disqualifications can affect the accountability of the government. It is essential for the government to take measures to prevent vacancies in the parliament and ensure the smooth functioning of the government.
