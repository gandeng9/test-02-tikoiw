Introduction

Are you considering a career in commercial truck driving, but don't have any prior experience? If so, you're not alone. Many job seekers are interested in CDL (Commercial Driver's License) jobs but don't know where to start. The good news is that there are plenty of companies that hire drivers with no experience. In this article, we'll explore some of the options available to you.

Why Choose a Career in Truck Driving?

Truck driving can be a lucrative career choice for those who enjoy being on the road and don't mind spending long hours away from home. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heavy and tractor-trailer truck drivers was $45,260 as of May 2021. Additionally, the demand for truck drivers is expected to grow in the coming years, meaning that job opportunities should be plentiful.

CDL Requirements

Before we dive into the job options available to those with no experience, it's important to understand the CDL requirements. To obtain a CDL, you must pass a written knowledge test and a skills test. The skills test includes a pre-trip inspection, a basic control skills test, and an on-road driving test. The specific requirements may vary by state, so be sure to check with your local DMV.

Job Options for CDL Drivers with No Experience

1. Company-Sponsored Training Programs

Many trucking companies offer training programs that allow you to earn your CDL while being paid. These programs typically last a few weeks and include both classroom and on-the-road training. After completing the program, you'll be hired by the company and can begin your career as a truck driver.

2. Local Delivery Jobs

Another option for those with no experience is to look for local delivery jobs. These jobs typically involve driving a smaller truck or van and making deliveries within a specific area. While the pay may be lower than long-haul trucking jobs, these positions can be a good way to gain experience and build your skills.

3. Owner-Operator Jobs

If you're interested in running your own business, becoming an owner-operator may be a good option. As an owner-operator, you'll own your own truck and be responsible for finding your own clients. While this can be a challenging job, it can also be very rewarding.

4. Team Driving

Team driving involves driving with a partner and sharing the driving responsibilities. This can be a good option for those with no experience, as you'll have someone to help you navigate the road and share the workload.

Tips for Finding a CDL Job with No Experience

1. Network

One of the best ways to find a CDL job with no experience is to network with other drivers. Reach out to local trucking associations or attend job fairs to meet other drivers and learn about job opportunities.

2. Be Willing to Start Small

As mentioned earlier, local delivery jobs can be a good way to gain experience. Don't be afraid to start small and work your way up to longer hauls or owner-operator jobs.

3. Be Patient

Finding a CDL job with no experience may take some time, so be patient and persistent in your job search. Keep your resume updated and be prepared to apply to multiple positions before finding the right fit.

Conclusion

In conclusion, there are plenty of options for those interested in CDL jobs but don't have any prior experience. Company-sponsored training programs, local delivery jobs, owner-operator positions, and team driving are all viable options to consider. Remember to be patient in your job search and don't be afraid to start small. With hard work and dedication, you can build a successful career in commercial truck driving.
