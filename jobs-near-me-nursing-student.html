Jobs Near Me For Nursing Students In 2023

Introduction

As a nursing student, finding a job that is close to home can be challenging. However, with the current job market, there are plenty of opportunities available for nursing students who are looking to gain experience and earn some extra cash. In this article, we will explore the best jobs available for nursing students near their homes.

1. Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA)

One of the most popular jobs for nursing students is a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA). CNAs work in hospitals, nursing homes, and other healthcare facilities. They provide basic patient care, such as taking vital signs, assisting with activities of daily living, and helping patients with mobility. CNAs can earn an average hourly wage of $13.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 11% in the next ten years.

2. Home Health Aide

Another job that is in high demand is a Home Health Aide. Home Health Aides work with patients who need assistance with daily living activities but prefer to stay at home instead of going to a nursing home or hospital. They assist with personal hygiene, meal preparation, medication management, and other tasks. Home Health Aides can earn an hourly wage of $12.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 36% in the next ten years.

3. Medical Scribe

If you are interested in gaining experience in the medical field, becoming a Medical Scribe is a great option. Medical Scribes work alongside doctors and nurses to document patient encounters. They take notes on the patient's medical history, symptoms, and treatments. Medical Scribes can earn an hourly wage of $15.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 17% in the next ten years.

4. Patient Care Technician

A Patient Care Technician is another job that is great for nursing students. They work in hospitals and nursing homes, providing basic patient care. They assist with bathing, dressing, feeding, and other daily activities. Patient Care Technicians can earn an hourly wage of $14.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 8% in the next ten years.

5. Medical Receptionist

If you are looking for a job that is more administrative, becoming a Medical Receptionist is a great option. Medical Receptionists work in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare facilities. They greet patients, schedule appointments, and handle administrative tasks. Medical Receptionists can earn an hourly wage of $13.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 9% in the next ten years.

6. Medical Assistant

A Medical Assistant is another job that is in high demand. Medical Assistants work in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare facilities. They assist with patient care, such as taking vital signs and administering medications. They also handle administrative tasks, such as scheduling appointments and billing. Medical Assistants can earn an hourly wage of $15.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 19% in the next ten years.

7. Pharmacy Technician

If you are interested in the pharmaceutical industry, becoming a Pharmacy Technician is a great option. Pharmacy Technicians work in pharmacies, hospitals, and other healthcare facilities. They assist pharmacists with filling prescriptions, managing inventory, and handling administrative tasks. Pharmacy Technicians can earn an hourly wage of $14.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 12% in the next ten years.

8. Dental Assistant

If you are interested in dentistry, becoming a Dental Assistant is a great option. Dental Assistants work in dental offices, assisting dentists with patient care. They take x-rays, prepare patients for treatments, and handle administrative tasks. Dental Assistants can earn an hourly wage of $16.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 11% in the next ten years.

9. Personal Care Aide

If you prefer to work with elderly or disabled patients, becoming a Personal Care Aide is a great option. Personal Care Aides work in nursing homes, assisted living facilities, and private homes. They assist with personal hygiene, meal preparation, medication management, and other tasks. Personal Care Aides can earn an hourly wage of $12.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 36% in the next ten years.

10. Nurse

If you are close to completing your nursing degree, becoming a Nurse is a great option. Nurses work in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare facilities. They provide patient care, administer medications, and handle administrative tasks. Nurses can earn an hourly wage of $30.50, and the job outlook is expected to grow by 15% in the next ten years.

Conclusion

In conclusion, there are plenty of job opportunities available for nursing students near their homes. Whether you prefer to work in a hospital, nursing home, or private home, there is a job that is perfect for you. By exploring the options listed above, you can gain valuable experience, earn some extra cash, and prepare for your future career in the healthcare industry.
